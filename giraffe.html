GIRAFFE

Giraffes are the world's tallest mammals, thanks to their towering legs and long necks. A giraffe's legs alone are taller than many humansâ€”about 6 feet . These long legs allow giraffes to run as fast as 35 miles an hour over short distances and cruise comfortably at 10 miles an hour over longer distances.

Giraffe Behavior. Giraffes are social animals. Female giraffes associate in groups called herds, occasionally including a few younger males. Mature males live in "bachelor" herds and fight for dominance using their necks. older males are usually solitary. Giraffes feed and drink during the morning and evening.
